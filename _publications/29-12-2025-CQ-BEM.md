In this paper, we investigate the application of a Lubich-based convolution quadrature combined with
collocation boundary element methods for 3D exterior time-domain scattering problems involving sound-hard
obstacles. We explore both nodal and ε-collocation strategies, focusing on their accuracy, efficiency and
stability. Numerical experiments demonstrate accuracy comparable to that of the Lubich-Galerkin method
and assess the unconditional stability of the collocation scheme, provided that boundary integrals are
evaluated with sufficient precision.

**Keywords:** 3D wave equation, Neumann problem, space-time boundary integral
equation, convolution quadrature, collocation methods
